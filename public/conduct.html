Player code of conduct.

The Dink Society is competitive but social. Three pillars hold the whole thing up.

Last updated: April 2026
Respect
Opponents, partners, refs, facility staff, neighbors.
Honesty
Calls, scores, line calls. Always.
Joy
Show up to compete, but stay for the post-match hang.

1. On the court

  • Make line calls in good faith. If in doubt, the ball is in.
  • Call your own foot faults at the kitchen line.
  • Acknowledge great shots — including your opponents'.
  • Keep the volume up for cheering, down for arguing.
  • Never throw a paddle or strike the net or fence in frustration.

2. With opponents

  • No trash talk. Friendly banter is fine; personal attacks are not.
  • Shake hands, tap paddles, or fist-bump after every match.
  • If a dispute arises, both captains pause play and resolve it calmly.
  • If you can't agree, replay the point and move on.

3. With your partner

  • Communicate constantly — calls, switches, encouragement.
  • Take responsibility for your own mistakes; don't pin them on your partner.
  • If you need a moment to reset, take it between points, not during.

4. With the facility & community

  • Keep noise reasonable — many of our courts are in residential neighborhoods.
  • Pack out everything you brought in. Leave the courts cleaner than you found them.
  • Park considerately. Don't block driveways or fire lanes.
  • Respect open-play schedules at public facilities.

5. Off the court

  • What happens at the post-match hang stays friendly.
  • Don't post photos or video of other players without their okay.
  • If you bring drinks, drink responsibly. Designated drivers, please.
  • Treat the league's social channels and group chats with the same respect you bring to the court.

6. Zero tolerance

The following result in immediate removal from a match and may lead to suspension or expulsion from the league without refund:

  • Physical aggression toward another player, official, or spectator.
  • Slurs or harassment based on race, gender, sexuality, religion, or any protected status.
  • Intentionally hitting another player with a ball or paddle.
  • Cheating — including knowingly miscalling lines or scores after they've been corrected.

7. Reporting

If you experience or witness a violation, report it to your captain or directly to the league at hi@thedinksociety.com. We take every report seriously and respond within 48 hours.

8. Spirit of the league

This is rec pickleball. It's supposed to be hard, competitive, and fun all at once. If you're ever unsure whether something crosses a line, ask. We'd rather have an awkward conversation than lose a player or an opponent over an avoidable misstep.

Questions about this code? Email hi@thedinksociety.com.
